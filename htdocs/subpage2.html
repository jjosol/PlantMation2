Gymnosperms

Species:
Tsuga heterophylla

Common Name:
Western hemlock

Water needed:
Regular watering

Location:
Northwestern limit on the Kenai Peninsula, Alaska, and its southeastern limit in northern Sonoma County, California.

Species:
Cycas revoluta

Common Name:
Sago Palm

Water needed:
Water once the soil begins to dry slightly at the top

Location:
Southern Japan

Species:
Gnetum africanum

Common Name:
Wild spinach

Water needed:
Once a week

Location:
In the humid tropical forest regions of Central African Republic, Cameroon, Gabon, Democratic Republic of the Congo and Angola

Species:
Ginkgo biloba

Common Name:
Maidenhair tree

Water needed:
Rainfall should be adequate

Location:
China

Species:
Sequoia sempervirens

Common Name:
Coast Redwood

Water needed:
Each tree would use about 10 gallons of water per day.

Location:
Location: It is native to moist, foggy, coastal plain areas along the Pacific coast, now being primarily confined to a narrow coastal belt (to 20 miles inland) extending from southern Oregon south to the San Francisco Bay area.

Species:
Pinus sylvestris

Common Name:
Scots Pine

Water needed:
Pines require about 1 inch of water each week from either rain or home irrigation

Location:
Is native to Eurasia, ranging from Western Europe to Eastern Siberia, south to the Caucasus Mountains and Anatolia, and north to well inside the Arctic Circle in Fennoscandia

Species:
Gnetum africanum

Common Name:
Hinoki Cypress

Water needed:
Rain, and they should only be watered during the drier months of summer or when the soil feels dry to a depth of 4 inches.

Location:
It is native to central Japan in East Asia

Species:
Metasequoia glyptostroboides

Common Name:
Dawn Redwood

Water needed:
Provide at least 1 inch of water weekly to the entire area under the branch canopy, which can be quite a large area

Location:
Native to Lichuan county in Hubei province, China.

Consistent watering

Butterfly pea

Regular watering

Naturally found in grassland, open woodland, bush, riverine vegetation, and disturbed places.

Cortaderia selloana

Common Name:
Pampas grass

Water needed:
Low to moderate

Location:
Low wet areas of Argentina and southern Brazil.

Species:
Aconitum plicatum

Common Name:
Garden Monkshood

Water needed:
It likes to be kept consistently moist but not waterlogged

Location:
Native to Europe (Germany, Czech Republic and Poland)

Species:
Portulacaria afra

Common Name:
Elephant Bush

Water needed:
Elephant bushes should be watered more often in summer and less in the winter. Let the soil dry between waterings, usually four to five days

Location:
Found in South Africa

Species:
Prunus serrulata

Common Name:
Japanese Cherry

Water needed:
Water thoroughly, until the soil is wet and a small puddle remains around the tree.

Location:
East Asia, and has only naturalized in a very few areas of North America.

Species:
Cinnamomum cassia

Common Name:
Chinese cassia

Water needed:
Water: Lightly soak the soil and then do not water again until the top 2 inches are dry this may total to a weekly or daily watering.

Location:
Native to the lowlands of southern China but cultivated throughout Southeast Asia
